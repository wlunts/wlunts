The Field Observation

This inspection was unique. Unlike our typical overseas client orders, this engagement was for a large-scale domestic B2C platform in China.

According to our inspector, this tea factory doesn’t just process leaves — they own the tea mountains and the tea trees. They control the process from the raw harvest at the bottom layer of the supply chain all the way through to the final packaging.

While tea hasn’t traditionally been the primary focus for international clients, the global appreciation for Chinese culture is growing, and tea culture is naturally riding that wave. On a personal note, I’m a huge tea enthusiast myself. So, I’ve decided to document this factory—a bit out of personal bias, and a bit as a tip for the ‘destined’ clients who might be looking for the real deal.

Why This Matters for Buyers

In the tea industry, the biggest risk to quality consistency is sourcing consistency.

When a factory buys leaves from various small farmers — which is common in tea production — they lose control over harvesting standards, pesticide usage, and initial fermentation quality. By owning the tea mountain, this factory ensures a stable baseline for their products.

A Professional Note for International Buyers

This factory does not have a dedicated foreign trade department.

  • The Challenge: Communication and export logistics will require a partner (a sourcing agent or logistics company) to bridge the gap.
  • The Advantage: You are dealing directly with the source. If you are willing to manage export compliance, you gain access to authentic, farm-direct pricing and quality that most “trading-type” factories cannot match.
